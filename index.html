Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about buying, selling, and investing in real estate

You're likely ready to buy if you have a stable income, a good credit score (typically 620+), enough savings for a down payment (usually 3–20%), and plan to stay in the area for at least 3–5 years. Speaking with one of our agents can help you assess your specific situation.

Spring (March–May) is traditionally the best time to sell, as buyer demand peaks and homes tend to sell faster and for more money. However, low inventory in winter can also work in your favor. Our team can advise based on your local market conditions.

Your offer should be based on recent comparable sales in the area, the home's condition, and current market competition. In a seller's market, offering at or above asking price may be necessary. EliteHomes agents provide a detailed market analysis to help you make the right offer.

Closing costs are fees paid at the end of a real estate transaction, typically 2–5% of the loan amount. They include lender fees, title insurance, appraisal, and taxes. Both buyers and sellers pay closing costs, though the split can be negotiated.
